I'm extremely passionate about helping the mentally ill in our society today. I want to erase this stigma around mental health that causes people to be labeled "crazy" and scared to come forward and receive the help they so desperately need. Instead of kicking them while they are down, we should be helping them back up. It's socially acceptable to take medicine for high blood pressure, yet isn't deemed okay by society to take medicine for the most important part of the human body: the brain. 800,000 people a year die from suicide every single year and I'm determined to lower that statistic. I've given speeches, organized on-campus awareness events, and have challenged peoples perspectives. However, that's not enough. Many of these people aren't lucky enough to come from a privileged socioeconomic background. While they are just trying to recover from their illnesses many are faced with the daunting prospect of finding their next meal or a roof over their heads.
I aim to merge my passions in Computer Science and passion to help the "outcasts" of our society by creating technology to help those in rehabilitation facilities access the support they need easier. Accessing resources such as homeless shelters, food banks, partial hospitalization programs, and support groups like AA are NA are objectively much more difficult to do without access to internet, laptops, or technological conveniences that we take for granted. There are many other solutions that could be created that could streamline and improve the rehabilitation process. I believe that our society is able to change in a positive direction and I plan to facilitate that for the rest of my life.
