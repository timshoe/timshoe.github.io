About Me

Hi, I'm Tim. I'm a Computer Science major at the Georgia Insitute of Technology in the Class of 2020.

I've always been fascinated with technology starting at a young age. At five years old, my brother’s Gameboy entranced me with reveries of defeating Pokemon and exploring foreign worlds. At ten, I marveled at my first iPod; it was remarkably thin, light, and easy to use. I was amazed at the idea that in just a few years, new technology would make such a cutting edge product obsolete. I am inspired by computer science, something that has vast applications in dozens of fields and the potential to dramatically influence the futures of other areas.

I hope to explore opportunities and make an impact within a variety of different industries, through technologies such as artificial intelligence and big data.

Outside of my studies, I work as a student assistant at the Georgia Tech College of Computing. I am also actively involved in my school in ways such as being Corporate Outreach Chair for the Association of Computing Machinery and volunteering to help create sustainable energy through Engineers without Borders.
